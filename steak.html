Butter, Garlic, and Rosemary basted Steak

Description

A butter, garlic, and rosemary basted steak is a steak that is seared on a cast iron pan over medium
high heat for a time depending on the chefs choice. It has a rich meaty flavor with a juiciness that
is assisted by the basting of the butter, garlic, and rosemary.

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil
  • Cast Iron Skillet
  • Ribeye Steak
  • Butter
  • Rosemary
  • Garlic

Steps

  1. Leave out steak for 30 minutes if not yet at room temperature, if at room temperature proceed to next step
  2. Season steak with seasonings of choice
  3. Heat up cast iron skillet on medium high heat until smoke is coming off of skillet
  4. Place steak on hot cast iron pan and leave for 4 minutes and 30 seconds for medium rare
  5. Once time has passed flip steak and after 2 minutes place slices of butter, garlic, and rosemary in pan
  6. Use spoon to baste melted butter, garlic, and rosemary on seared side of steak
  7. Once steak is reaching preferred temperature flip again and baste other side
  8. When steak reaches preferred temperature pull out and let it rest for 5 minutes
